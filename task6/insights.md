# Insights

## Monthly Revenue Analysis

- Monthly revenue was calculated using the `SUM()` aggregate function.
- Revenue varied across different months, indicating changes in sales performance over time.

## Order Volume Analysis

- Monthly order volume was calculated using `COUNT(DISTINCT order_id)`.
- The results show how the number of customer orders changed from month to month.

## Product Revenue Analysis

- Revenue generated by each product was calculated using `SUM(amount)`.
- Some products contributed significantly more revenue than others.

## Average Revenue

- The average monthly revenue was calculated using the `AVG()` function.
- This provides an overview of the average sales performance across different months.

## Top Performing Months

- The top three months with the highest revenue were identified using `ORDER BY` and `LIMIT`.
- These months can be used to understand peak sales periods.

## Conclusion

SQL aggregation functions such as `SUM()`, `COUNT()`, `AVG()`, `GROUP BY`, `ORDER BY`, and `LIMIT` were used to analyze sales trends, order volume, and product performance. The analysis provides useful business insights that support data-driven decision-making.
