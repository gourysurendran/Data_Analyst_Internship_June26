# Insights

## Dataset Overview
- The Titanic dataset contains **891 passenger records** with **12 features**.
- It includes demographic information, ticket details, passenger class, fare, and survival status.

## Key Findings

- Missing values were found in the **Age**, **Cabin**, and **Embarked** columns.
- Most passengers were **young adults**.
- The dataset contains **more male passengers than female passengers**.
- The **Fare** column contains several high-value outliers.
- There is **no strong relationship** between passenger age and fare.
- Most numerical variables have **weak correlations**.
- Passengers traveling in **higher classes** and those who paid **higher fares** generally had **better survival rates**.

## Visual Analysis

- The histogram showed the distribution of passenger ages.
- The boxplot highlighted outliers in the Fare column.
- The scatter plot compared Age and Fare.
- The correlation heatmap displayed relationships among numerical features.
- The pairplot provided a comprehensive comparison of important variables and survival patterns.

## Conclusion

The Exploratory Data Analysis helped identify missing values, understand feature distributions, detect outliers, and explore relationships between variables. These insights provide a better understanding of the Titanic dataset and demonstrate the importance of EDA before building machine learning models.
